Annie Easley Timeline

Timeline

1933

Mathematician Annie J. Easley was born on April 23, 1933 in Birmingham, Alabama to Samuel and Mary Easley. Easley grew up in Birmingham and initially sought to be a nurse, but she soon switched to pharmacy once she started high school.

1951-54

Easley entered Xavier University in New Orleans, Louisiana in 1951. After completing 2 years of study she returned to Birmingham to marry a U.S. military man in 1954 and moved to Cleveland, Ohio where her husbandâ€™s family was located.

1958

Easley read an article by NASA in the newspaper saying that individuals with strong mathematics skills were needed at the laboratory. Easley immediately applied and two weeks later began her 34 year-long career with NASA as a computer scientist and mathematician.

1962

Easley was but one of four African American employees in the computational section and was on the front line of space research and subsequent space missions that began with the launch of astronaut John Glenn into orbit in 1962.

1963

During the early 1960s, Easley began working on nuclear-powered rocket systems including the Centaur high-energy booster rocket, which had its first successful launch in 1963.

1945

Easley returned to school in the 1970s to complete a mathematics degree from Cleveland State University in 1977, while working full time.

2011

Annie J. Easley died on June 25, 2011, at the age 78. Her legacy continues to inspire countless students to make an impact in the STEM field.
